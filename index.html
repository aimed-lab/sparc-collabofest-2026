The Challenge

From biological insight to an asset you can validate.

CollaboFest is designed to move promising research — a mechanism, a clinical observation, an accessible dataset — toward an experimentally testable therapeutic solution, generating pilot evidence for grants, publications, partnerships, and potential UAB intellectual property. Teams submit a concise three-page proposal through the CollaboFest portal.

1

Form a team & frame the decision

Assemble a multidisciplinary team that owns the science and a credible path to validation. Name the single decision, prediction, or asset you need SPARC to produce.

≥ 1 UAB / CCTS co-investigator
2

Submit a three-page proposal

Describe the unmet need, central hypothesis, scientific foundation, the SPARC support requested, your team readiness, and a feasible validation plan — through the designated portal.

Portal submission required
3

Review, award & scope

Agent-assisted analysis supports a judging panel that recommends winners. Three teams jointly define a focused scope of SPARC services with SPARC.

Three Pillars

A proposal may begin at one pillar — or connect all three.

Projects may address Targets, Chemicals, or Patient Cohorts, or an integrated end-to-end path that connects mechanism, therapeutic design, validation, and the patients most likely to benefit.

TargetsPillar I

Discover what to treatMechanism → target → biomarker

Identify and prioritize a disease mechanism, candidate target, pathway, cell type, biomarker, or modality-selection question using AI and systems pharmacology.

Illustrative deliverables
  • Ranked target portfolio
  • Target-evidence dossier
  • Mechanism map
  • Modality recommendation
ChemicalsPillar II

Design what to giveScreen → generate → optimize

Move from a small molecule, antibody, biologic, or repositioning opportunity toward a prioritized, testable set of candidates through virtual screening and generative design.

Illustrative deliverables
  • Prioritized synthesis list
  • Novel candidate structures
  • Repurposing package
  • Candidate dossier
Patient CohortsPillar III

Design who & howPhenotype → enrich → simulate

Define the right population — responder signatures, digital twins, synthetic cohorts, and virtual trials — to explain variable response and de-risk translation.

Illustrative deliverables
  • Computable cohort definition
  • Responder signature
  • Trial-simulation report
  • Dose / endpoint rec.
End-to-end

The strongest submissions often connect the pillars — turning a published mechanism into a candidate shortlist and an assay-ready validation package for a molecularly defined patient group.

What the $20,000 includes

An idea-generating menu, not a fixed package.

Each selected project receives a jointly defined scope of SPARC services valued at up to $20,000. A practical project usually emphasizes one primary service module and a few supporting analyses. During scoping, SPARC and the team agree on the question, inputs, deliverables, validation handoff, and stopping point.

Generally outside the award

Unless explicitly scoped, awards do not cover compound synthesis or purchase, wet-lab experiments, animal studies, clinical recruitment, IRB or regulatory submissions, patent fees, or third-party costs. SPARC can help design and connect these downstream activities.

Who can enter

Built on the UAB-led CCTS Partner Network.

Every team needs a scientific home in the network — but the rest of your team can come from anywhere.

Core eligibility requirement

At least one co-investigator from UAB or the CCTS Partner Network

Each team must include a co-investigator whose primary institutional affiliation is either the University of Alabama at Birmingham (UAB) or an institution in the UAB-led Center for Clinical and Translational Science (CCTS) Partner Network. Additional members may come from other institutions or organizations, subject to program requirements.

Who should form a team

Complementary assets beyond informatics.

  • Scientific or clinical ownershipA disease-mechanism study, evidence-supported target shortlist, or clinical expertise, cohorts, and endpoints.
  • An accessible dataset or validation platformA unique patient-cohort or experimental dataset, distinctive assay, organoid, or new approach methodology (NAM).
  • Translational & development reachMedicinal chemistry, biologics, pharmacology, regulatory, IP, or a commercial / external translational partner.
  • Committed resourcesExisting external funding or committed resources related to the proposed program. No team is expected to hold every asset.
The Three-Page Proposal

Seven things every proposal should make clear.

The most useful proposals name the decision to be made, the evidence already available, the SPARC work needed, the intended deliverable, and the experiment or translational step that follows.

01

Unmet need

The disease problem or critical translational barrier.

02

Central hypothesis

The target, mechanism, intervention, or patient-selection concept to test.

03

Scientific foundation

Key publications, preliminary findings, clinical observations, and data.

04

Proposed solution

The decision, prediction, design, or translational asset you want to produce.

05

SPARC support requested

The specific services needed — and why they are essential.

06

Team readiness

Expertise, datasets, assays, models, clinical resources, partners, and funding.

07

Validation plan & next milestone

A feasible experimental, computational, clinical, or translational test of the output — and how success supports a grant, manuscript, invention disclosure, licensing opportunity, startup, partnership, or clinical study. Candidate-generating projects should identify a wet-lab validation route.

How proposals are reviewed

Agent-assisted analysis. Human judgment decides.

SPARC uses automated, agent-assisted reviews as decision support to keep preliminary assessment consistent — they do not independently select winners. A panel of judges reviews the evidence and recommends the final awards.

◆

Significance & impact

Of the unmet need and translational potential.

◆

Rigor & innovation

Strength of rationale and differentiation from existing approaches.

◆

Fit with SPARC

Alignment with AI and systems-pharmacology capabilities.

◆

Feasibility

Within the available service award and project period.

◆

Data quality

Quality and accessibility of the required data.

◆

Validation credibility

And clear ownership of the follow-up work.

Judges represent SPARC, the UAB Department of Biomedical Informatics and Data Science (DBIDS), the Marnix E. Heersink Institute for Biomedical Innovation, Southern Research, and the UAB-led CCTS. Criteria for the Grand Prize will be announced separately.

Questions

What teams ask most.

Is the award cash?

+
No. Each of the three awards is up to $20,000 in scoped, in-kind SPARC drug-discovery services — commitments of expertise and computation, not a cash grant. SPARC and each selected team jointly define the scope, deliverables, responsibilities, milestones, and timeline.

Who has to be on the team?

+
At least one co-investigator whose primary institutional affiliation is UAB or an institution in the UAB-led CCTS Partner Network. Other members may come from any institution or organization. See the CCTS Partner Network list.

What do we submit, and how?

+
A concise three-page proposal covering the unmet need, central hypothesis, scientific foundation, proposed solution, SPARC support requested, team readiness, validation plan, and next milestone. Submission is through the designated CollaboFest collaboration portal and is required for all contestants.

What does the award not cover?

+
Unless specifically included in the final scope, awards should not be assumed to cover compound synthesis or purchase, wet-lab experiments, animal studies, clinical recruitment, IRB or regulatory submissions, patent expenses, or third-party fees. SPARC may help design or connect these downstream activities under a separate agreement.

How are winners chosen?

+
SPARC uses automated, agent-assisted reviews as decision support for consistent preliminary assessment. A panel of judges from SPARC, DBIDS, the Heersink Institute, Southern Research, and the UAB-led CCTS reviews the evidence and recommends the final winners. Criteria for the Grand Prize will be announced separately.

Who owns the intellectual property?

+
Teams should identify relevant background IP, prior disclosures, restricted data, and potential inventorship considerations. Data access, privacy, security, publication, authorship, and IP matters follow applicable UAB policies and project-specific agreements.
